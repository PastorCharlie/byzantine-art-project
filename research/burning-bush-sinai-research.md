# Research Note — Moses and the Burning Bush, Saint Catherine's Sinai (12th c.)

**Slug:** `burning-bush-sinai`
**Collection:** OT Typology only — single collection. The reflection's center is the Christophanic Name-of-God reading (Exodus 3:14 → John 8:58). No other collection is a clean fit.
**Compiled:** 2026-04-29

## 1. Identification

- **Object:** A 12th-century panel icon depicting Moses turning aside to view the burning bush. Composition: Moses on the right, looking back over his shoulder; his staff on the ground; his shoes removed (per Exodus 3:5); the burning bush in flame at the upper left, with a hand of God emerging from it (a Byzantine iconographic convention for divine speech). Sometimes additional elements appear in extended versions: Mount Horeb behind, sheep at the base, etc.
- **Medium:** Tempera and gold on wood panel.
- **Dimensions:** 92 × 64 cm.
- **Date:** 12th century (possibly extending into early 13th). Late Komnenian period.
- **Location:** Saint Catherine's Monastery, Sinai Peninsula, Egypt. Same site as Sinai Pantocrator (#2), Sinai apse Transfiguration (#14), Sinai Virgin and Child (#24), Sinai Annunciation (#29). The fifth Sinai entry in the corpus.
- **Geographical theological note:** The monastery sits at the traditional location of the burning bush event — a "burning bush" plant (Rubus sanctus) is preserved on the monastery grounds and venerated as a descendant of the original. The icon was painted on-site, depicting the event in the place where Christian tradition holds it occurred.
- **Era:** Middle Byzantine.
- **Period:** Middle Byzantine, Komnenian.

Key contextual facts that shaped the reflection:
- **Exodus 3:14 LXX → NT.** The Hebrew "*ehyeh asher ehyeh*" (אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה) is rendered in the LXX as "ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ὤν" (I am the one being). When Christ says "ἐγώ εἰμι" in John 8:58 (and his absolute "ego eimi" sayings throughout John), he is claiming the divine Name. This Greek-to-Greek connection is unmistakable in the NT, and the Pharisees' reaction (picking up stones, John 8:59) confirms they understood the claim.
- **Acts 7:30–35 Stephen's sermon.** Stephen recounts the burning bush event and explicitly identifies the speaker as "the angel of the Lord" (v. 30). This is the key NT pattern of "angel of the LORD" Christophanies — the pre-incarnate Word appearing in the OT before taking flesh.
- **The Marian-bush typology.** Patristic and medieval (especially Eastern) tradition developed a typology where the burning bush = Mary's womb (bush burning but not consumed = Mary bearing God without being destroyed). This is not NT-affirmed and falls under the locked-in Coll 6 rule of "the corpus affirms a type only when a NT author affirmed it first." The reflection explicitly rejects this typology while honoring what the NT does affirm.
- **The Christophanic doubling with Rublev (#38).** This is the second consecutive Coll 6 entry centered on a pre-incarnation Christophany — Mamre's "third visitor" (Rublev) and Sinai's "angel of the LORD" (this entry). The pattern emerging across Coll 6: OT theophanic appearances are most defensibly read as Christophanies, not as direct Father-or-Spirit appearances and not as full Trinitarian revelations.

## 4. Scripture verification (KJV)

**Exodus 3:1–15** (the burning bush narrative). Reflection quotes selectively from vv. 2, 4, 5: "the angel of the LORD appeared unto him in a flame of fire out of the midst of a bush... God called unto him out of the midst of the bush, and said, Moses, Moses... put off thy shoes from off thy feet, for the place whereon thou standest is holy ground." **Verified.**

**Exodus 3:14:** "And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you." Reflection cites the divine Name. **Verified.**

**Acts 7:30:** "And when forty years were expired, there appeared to him in the wilderness of mount Sina an angel of the Lord in a flame of fire in a bush." **Verified.** Stephen's identification of the speaker as "an angel of the Lord."

**John 8:58:** "Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Before Abraham was, I am." **Verified.** Christ's claim of the Exodus 3:14 divine Name in present tense.

**Mark 12:26–27** (in `scripture_refs`): "And as touching the dead, that they rise: have ye not read in the book of Moses, how in the bush God spake unto him, saying, I am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob? He is not the God of the dead, but the God of the living." **Verified.** Christ's use of the burning bush passage to prove the resurrection. Not quoted in reflection but underlies the broader Christological reading of the bush.
