# Research Note — Christ Enthroned with Archangels, San Vitale (Ravenna)

**Slug:** `christ-archangels-san-vitale` | **Compiled:** 2026-04-29 (batch 012)

## Quick reference
- **Object:** Apse mosaic of San Vitale, Ravenna (consecrated 547 under Bishop Maximian). Youthful beardless Christ enthroned on the cosmic globe, holding the seven-sealed scroll (Rev 5:1). Archangels Michael and Gabriel flank him (right and left). Saint Vitalis (right) and Bishop Ecclesius (left) at the perimeter.
- **Image:** Petar Milošević 2015, CC BY-SA 4.0, downsampled 4545×2638 → 4000×2322 via script.

## Calibration — activates BRIEF Coll 3 #17 substitution
**This entry executes the BRIEF substitution Pastor Charlie made earlier.** BRIEF originally said "Christ enthroned with archangels, Sant'Apollinare in Classe" (#17 in Coll 3) — that was iconographically inaccurate (Classe shows a cosmic cross, not Christ enthroned). Pastor Charlie corrected to San Vitale. This entry activates that correction.

- **Cross-tag `second-coming`** earned: Christ holds the seven-sealed scroll (Rev 5:1); the seals release the eschatological judgments of Revelation 6+. The youthful Christ holds the eschaton in his hand, ready to break the seals. Archangels are at the ready position to be dispatched.
- **Ministering-not-mediating articulated iconographically:** the archangels are *at Christ's elbows*, not between Christ and the saints. Vitalis receives directly from Christ; Ecclesius offers directly to Christ. The angels frame the encounter; they do not mediate it. Hebrews 1:14 + 1 Tim 2:5 anchor.
- **Four-level architectural staging:** floor (worshipper) / saints (church-historical) / archangels (heavenly court) / Christ (throne). The Byzantine artist staged the order of approach.
- **Iconographic Christ-type note:** youthful beardless Christ — early Byzantine pre-Pantocrator type. Same type as #13 Sant'Apollinare Nuovo Christ Enthroned (also Justinianic). The bearded Pantocrator type would stabilize after c. 565.
