# Research Note — The Four Evangelists, Rabbula Gospels

**Slug:** `four-evangelists-rabbula` | **Compiled:** 2026-04-29 (batch 011)

## Quick reference
- **Object:** Folios 9v–10r of the Rabbula Gospels — illuminated portraits of the four evangelists flanking Eusebian canon tables. Syriac manuscript completed February 6, 586 AD by the scribe Rabbula at the Monastery of Saint John of Beth Zagba in Mesopotamia.
- **Image:** `Rabbula_Gospels_(9v_&_10r;_Four_Evangelists).jpg` (PD-Art, 2790×1880).
- **Current location:** Biblioteca Medicea Laurenziana, Florence (the manuscript was acquired by Cosimo de' Medici's agents in the 16th century; has remained in Florence since).

## Calibration
- **Coll 4 evangelist-symbol rule applied** (locked pre-batch 011 in voice-templates.md):
  - Each symbol read **Christologically**, not as primary identification of the evangelist.
  - Matthew/man → Son of Man (genealogy)
  - Mark/lion → Lion of Judah (royal proclamation)
  - Luke/ox → sacrificial offering (temple-altar imagery)
  - John/eagle → Logos (high-soaring)
- **Tier 1 Patristic citation: Irenaeus.** The evangelist-symbol typology comes from *Against Heresies* III.11.8 (c. 180 AD). Irenaeus is Tier 1 — earliest reliable, taught by Polycarp who was taught by John. Citation does historical-witness work (when the typology was formed) and witnesses to the apostolic-window context.
- **Eusebian canon tables** named contextually — the cross-reference system Eusebius developed c. 320 AD; the Rabbula scribe used them as the structural framework around which he placed the evangelist portraits. This subtly anchors the Tier 2 Patristic reference (Eusebius) without making him a doctrinal authority.
