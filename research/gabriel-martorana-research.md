# Research Note — Archangel Gabriel, La Martorana (Palermo)

**Slug:** `gabriel-martorana` | **Compiled:** 2026-04-29 (batch 012)

## Quick reference
- **Object:** Gabriel from the Annunciation mosaic at La Martorana / Santa Maria dell'Ammiraglio in Palermo. Mid-stride pose (foot lifted, himation flaring), originally part of an Annunciation pair flanking the entrance arch.
- **Image:** Marie-Lan Nguyen 2008, CC BY 2.5, 2125×2600.
- **Site:** La Martorana — built 1143 by George of Antioch (admiral of Norman Sicilian fleet under Roger II). Norman Sicilian Byzantine craftsmen. New site for the corpus.

## Calibration (substitution from original Coll 8 #3 plan — Annunciation Gabriel at Sinai already covered at #29)
- **Ministering-not-mediating articulated through motion.** Gabriel mid-stride, mid-message — angel iconography of *obedience.* He is on his way, not stopped to receive prayer. The motion is the doctrine.
- **Luke 1:19 grammar pivot:** *"I am Gabriel, that stand in the presence of God; and am sent to shew thee these glad tidings."* Two angelic postures named: standing in the presence (domestic, attending the throne) and being sent (missional). Both are about the One served, not the angel.
- **Architectural staging of incarnation noted:** at La Martorana the Annunciation pair originally flanked the entrance arch — the worshipper passed under the announcement as it happened.
- **Cross-tag `theotokos`** earned: Annunciation iconography belongs structurally in Coll 2.
- **Closing imperative:** *"Deliver the line. Then go."* — direct application of *"And the angel departed from her"* (Luke 1:38).
