# Research Note — Christ Pantocrator, Pammakaristos Church (Parekklesion Dome)

**Slug:** `pantocrator-pammakaristos`
**Collection:** Primary = Pantocrator. Secondary = Second Coming — the funerary chapel context and the 1 Cor 15 / Romans 14:9 framing of the reflection are pointed at resurrection eschatology, which is the Collection 3 territory. Earned, not padded.
**Compiled:** 2026-04-28

---

## 1. Identification

- **Object:** Christ Pantocrator, bust-length figure in the central medallion of the dome of the parekklesion (side chapel) of the Pammakaristos Church. Twelve Old Testament prophets ring the drum below: Moses, Jeremiah, Zephaniah, Micah, Joel, Zechariah, Obadiah, Habakkuk, Jonah, Malachi, Ezekiel, and Isaiah.
- **Medium:** Mosaic, glass tesserae on gold ground.
- **Date:** c. 1310–1320, Palaeologan period. The parekklesion itself was built c. 1304 as a funerary chapel.
- **Funerary context:** The chapel was commissioned by Maria Doukaina Komnene Branaina (widow of Protostrator Michael Tarchaneiotes Glabas, d. c. 1304) as a tomb chapel for her husband. The mosaic program is therefore a *funerary* program — Christ as Lord over the grave, prophets as witnesses to the resurrection.
- **Location:** Pammakaristos Church / Fethiye Camii, Çarşamba neighborhood, Fatih district, Istanbul, Turkey. Approx. 41.0299° N, 28.9469° E. The main church became Fethiye Mosque under Sultan Murad III (1591) and remains a mosque; the parekklesion has been a museum since 1949 restoration by the Byzantine Institute of America / Dumbarton Oaks.
- **Era:** Late Byzantine.
- **Period:** Palaeologan Renaissance — contemporary with and stylistically comparable to the Chora Church (Kariye) program.

## 5. Reflection authorship

Fresh draft. Distinguishing angle: **funerary**. Pammakaristos is the only Pantocrator in the batch with an explicit funerary program. The reflection turns on Christ-as-Lord-of-the-dead (Romans 14:9) and the resurrection promise (1 Cor 15). The prophets get a different reading here than at Daphni — there they were forward-pointing announcers of the Christ to come; here they are companions of the dead who themselves died in faith waiting (Heb 11:13). Same prophets, different theological work.

Sign-off not used. Closing imperative ("preach this Christ. The Lord is Lord on both sides of the threshold.") carries on its own.
