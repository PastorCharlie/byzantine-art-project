# Research Note — The Sacrifice of Isaac, San Vitale (Ravenna)

**Slug:** `sacrifice-isaac-san-vitale`
**Collection:** Primary = OT Typology (Coll 6 #6). Secondary = Second Coming (Coll 3) — earned: the substitutionary atonement reading is bound up with the eschatological "Lamb who was slain" of Revelation; Abraham's resurrection-faith (Heb 11:19) is itself eschatological.

## 1. Identification

- **Object:** Mosaic of the Sacrifice of Isaac in the lunette above the south wall of the presbytery of the Basilica of San Vitale, Ravenna. Composition: Abraham at center, raising a knife; Isaac kneeling on or beside a stone altar; a hand of God emerging from clouds at the upper right (the Byzantine convention for divine intervention/speech); a ram caught in a thicket of branches. The scene is paired with the Hospitality of Abraham (Genesis 18) on the same lunette as a continuous narrative panel — the visit of the three angels at Mamre flows directly into the binding of Isaac.
- **Sister panel:** The opposite (north) wall of the presbytery has another paired OT-typological lunette: Abel offering a lamb and Melchizedek offering bread/wine (Genesis 4 + Genesis 14), both prefiguring Christ's eucharistic sacrifice. The two lunettes together form a fourfold OT-eucharistic typology: Abel (the righteous offering), Melchizedek (the priest-king bread-and-wine offering), Abraham/Mamre (hospitality and divine visitation), Abraham/Isaac (substitutionary sacrifice).
- **Apse program (separate but related):** The apse of San Vitale shows Christ enthroned on a globe-throne, flanked by archangels Michael and Gabriel, with Saint Vitalis and Bishop Ecclesius. This apse is the artwork that should fill the slot BRIEF mistakenly placed at Sant'Apollinare in Classe (BRIEF Coll 3 #17 "Christ enthroned with archangels, Sant'Apollinare in Classe" — see batch 004 summary §3 for the BRIEF inaccuracy flag). When that slot gets filled, the entry will be a sister artwork to this one in the same basilica.
- **Date:** c. 547. The basilica was consecrated in 547 by Bishop Maximian of Ravenna. The mosaic program is roughly contemporary with consecration, executed during/after the Justinianic reconquest of Italy (Belisarius's Italian campaign 535–554).
- **Medium:** Wall mosaic on gold ground.
- **Location:** Basilica of San Vitale, Ravenna, Italy. UNESCO World Heritage Site (1996, "Early Christian Monuments of Ravenna"). Same UNESCO inscription as Sant'Apollinare Nuovo (#13, #28) and Sant'Apollinare in Classe (#18, #21). Approx. 44.4203° N, 12.1969° E.
- **Era:** Early Byzantine.
- **Period:** Early Byzantine, Justinianic.

Key contextual facts that shaped the reflection:
- **Hebrews 11:19's *en parabolē*.** The Greek phrase *ἐν παραβολῇ* (in parable / as a type) is the explicit NT typological-language used for Isaac's near-sacrifice. "From whence also he received him in a figure" (KJV) translates this. The author of Hebrews is doing the typology directly — Abraham's reception of Isaac back is a *parabolē* of the resurrection. This is one of the corpus's cleanest cases of NT-affirmed typology, satisfying Pastor Charlie's locked-in Coll 6 rule ("affirm a type only when a NT author affirmed it first").
- **Genesis 22:5 "we will come again."** Abraham's plural pronouns when leaving the servants ("I AND THE LAD will go yonder and worship, AND COME AGAIN to you") are exegetically load-bearing. Hebrews 11:19 explains the plural: Abraham was reasoning that God could raise Isaac from the dead, so both could return. This is resurrection-faith pre-cross.
- **The ram as substitute.** Genesis 22:13 explicitly names the ram as offered "in the stead of" Isaac. *Substitutionary atonement* is the doctrine the iconography stages.
- **John 3:16 echo.** "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son" reverses Genesis 22 typologically: Abraham was prepared to give his only beloved son and was stopped; God the Father gave his only beloved Son and was not stopped. The Patristic tradition reads John 3:16 as a deliberate inversion of Genesis 22 — though the NT does not explicitly cite this connection (only Hebrews 11 does the explicit typology). The reflection makes the John 3:16 echo without claiming it's a direct NT-affirmed type — it's an exegetical observation, not a type-claim.
- **Mount Moriah / Jerusalem temple / Calvary.** Jewish tradition (preserved in 2 Chronicles 3:1: "Then Solomon began to build the house of the LORD at Jerusalem in mount Moriah, where the LORD appeared unto David his father") identifies Mount Moriah of Genesis 22 with the Temple Mount in Jerusalem. Calvary is on the same Jerusalem ridge system. The geography is theological: the same mountain saw the Akedah and the Crucifixion. This is widely received in conservative Christian exegesis as a canonical-attestation-supported geographical insight.
