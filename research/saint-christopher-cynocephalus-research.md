## Quick reference

- **Object:** 17th-c. Eastern Greek icon, dog-headed Christopher type. Byzantine and Christian Museum, Athens (ΒΧΜ 01571). Public domain. 954×1773 px (converted from GIF).
- **Subject:** Saint Christopher with body of a man, head of a dog, holding palm branch and cross-staff.
- **Iconographic origin:** Likely a textual confusion — Latin *Cananeus* (Canaanite) misread as *caninus* (dog-like), conflated with classical-pagan *cynocephali* legends.

## Calibration decisions

- **Pastor Charlie's directional note for Christopher landed.** The reflection follows the suggested theological move: "the icon got the species wrong; the church got the saint wrong (as patron of travelers, not as authentic historical witness); but God is not mocked by misidentified icons — He receives the honor offered through them, even when the offering is confused."
- **Mode 4 with extra steps.** Christopher is the corpus's most extreme legend-rejection case. Three layers of decline:
  1. Decline the dog-head (iconographic error from textual confusion)
  2. Decline the giant-carrying-Christ-across-river story (13th-c. Western legend, no early attestation)
  3. Decline the Christopher-medallion-as-protection-from-death superstition (medieval cult)
- **Vatican removed Christopher from the universal calendar in 1969** because his historical existence couldn't be substantiated. The reflection notes this — it's relevant historical fact about how the saint has been handled even by traditions more sympathetic to his cult than GLM.
- **Honor the category, not the figure.** *"The category of Christ-bearer is real even when a specific named instance cannot be substantiated."* Many "Christ-bearers" (literal meaning of the Greek *Christophoros*) have died for the faith; Hebrews 11:36–38 and Revelation 6:9–11 attest the category. The category survives the failure of the specific historical case.
- **Pastoral generosity:** the reflection explicitly does NOT mock the believers who venerated this icon. *"They were doing the best they could with what their tradition handed them. God is not mocked by misidentified icons; He receives the honor offered through them when the heart was sincere, even when the iconography was confused."* This is essential — the corrective work cannot become contempt for sincere believers.
- **The pivotal pastoral move:** *"the worshipper's intent... does, however, reveal why we read scripture rather than tradition as our primary guide."* This is GLM's *sola scriptura* commitment named openly via a pastoral example.
