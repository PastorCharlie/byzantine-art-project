# Research Note — Saint Paul (Met Medallion, c. 1100)

**Slug:** `saint-paul-met-medallion` | **Compiled:** 2026-04-29 (batch 011)

## Quick reference
- **Object:** Cloisonné enamel medallion of Saint Paul, Constantinople workshop c. 1100. Currently at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York (gift of J. Pierpont Morgan, 1917; accession 17.190.673). Gold, silver, enamel; medallion 8.3 cm diameter.
- **Image:** `Medallion_with_Saint_Paul_from_an_Icon_Frame_MET_DT2810.jpg` (CC0, 3811×3049).

## Calibration
- **Consolidation rule application (locked batch 010):** Peter and Paul kept *separate* despite frequent iconographic pairing (*Concordia Apostolorum*). Their attributes differ (Paul with scroll/book; Peter with keys); their theological roles differ (apostle to the Gentiles vs. apostle to the Jews, Gal 2:7–8); their case-types differ (conversion-and-mission vs. denial-and-restoration). Two separate entries warranted.
- **Acts 7:58 / 8:1 pivot** opens the reflection: Paul's first NT appearance is consenting to Stephen's stoning. The man holding the cloaks would become the apostle whose own writing would be canonical scripture. Establishes the *anyone* doctrinal claim — anyone can be reached, remade, sent.
- **Iconographic theology centered on authorship.** Byzantine medallion shows Paul holding the books — the books he wrote — not depicted in a biographical-drama scene. The reflection makes this the iconographic move: Paul's foundational role is the canonical text he produced, not the biographical events.
- **Anti-Petrine-supremacy logic implicit** (developed explicitly at #51 Saint Peter Sinai). Paul's apostolic authority is parallel to Peter's, not subordinate. Eph 2:20 names *the apostles* in the plural; the foundation is apostolic, not Petrine specifically.
- **Sola scriptura pillar implicit** (locked in #51 Christopher): we read his thirteen letters as canonical scripture and treat his apostolic authority as foundational, while not directing devotion at the man.
