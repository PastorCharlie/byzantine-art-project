## Calibration
- **First Coll 4 entry on a single canonical apostle** (Paul at #54 was the same; Peter at #56 is the second).
- **Anti-Petrine-supremacy structural reading.** Pastor Charlie's directional note: keep Peter and Paul separate per the consolidation rule. Reflection makes the anti-supremacy point iconographically: the icon's *geometry* refuses Petrine supremacy by placing Peter beneath three medallions (Christ + Mary + John), with John explicitly on Peter's right. Peter is one apostle among others, derivative of Christ above. The Byzantine 6th-c. artist already saw the danger of Petrine elevation and corrected for it iconographically.
- **Three-fold biblical Peter sketched.** Confession (Matt 16:16) → eagerness (Matt 19:27) → denial (Mark 14:71) → restoration (John 21:15–17). This is a man, not a symbolic apostle.
- **Anti-papal explicit:** *"We particularly do not affirm the doctrine of papal supremacy that grew out of Petrine cult — Christ alone is the foundation of his church (1 Cor 3:11; Eph 2:20 names the apostles and prophets in the plural; the church is built on apostolic witness, not on a singular Petrine succession)."* This is the corpus's first explicit anti-papal-supremacy statement. Earned by the iconography forcing the question.
- **Restoration as the doctrine, not the denial.** Closing imperative: *"Preach him as the man who denied his Lord, was restored, fed the sheep, and died upside-down for the gospel. The restoration is the doctrine."* — pastoral application of the John 21:15–17 sequence.
