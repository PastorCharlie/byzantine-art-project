## Quick reference
- **Object:** Four pendentive seraphim mosaics below the central dome of Hagia Sophia. Six wings each, faces partly hidden by upper wing-pair (per Isaiah 6:2). 14th-c. Palaeologan restoration over earlier program.

## Calibration
- **Isaiah 6:1–3 anchor:** the architectural staging IS Isaiah 6 — pendentives at the corners between four arches and the dome, originally crowned with a Pantocrator. The mosaicists put the seraphim *exactly where Isaiah 6 said they were.*
- **Faces-hidden iconographic detail:** *"with twain he covered his face"* (Isaiah 6:2). The artist read the verse literally and rendered the wing-as-veil. Theological pivot: even the angels cover their faces; modesty is the angelic posture before God; *do not look at me; look at the One I attend.*
- **Closing imperative pattern (Coll 8 spec):** redirects from the angel to *the message the angel carries* / *the One the angel attends*. Reflection ends *"do what the angels do. Cover your face... Cry holy... Here am I; send me."* Direct application of Isaiah 6:6–8 to the reader.
