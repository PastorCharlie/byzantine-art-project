## 1. Identification

- **Object:** Mosaic of the Theotokos enthroned with the Christ-child on her lap, flanked by four angels (two on each side), with the three Magi advancing in offering posture in the foreground, on the lower register of the **north wall** of the nave at Sant'Apollinare Nuovo. The Magi are followed by a procession of 22 female martyrs/virgins extending the length of the wall, from the city of Classis (the harbor of Ravenna) to the throne.
- **Iconographic counterpart.** The opposite (south) wall shows Christ enthroned with four angels, receiving a procession of 26 male martyrs led by Saint Martin from Theodoric's palace (covered as #13 in our corpus). The two procession programs were designed in parallel — the male martyrs to Christ, the female martyrs and Magi to the Theotokos with the Christ-child. Together they stage the entire church (martyrs of both sexes, Gentile worshippers, angelic court) in worship of the incarnate Lord.
- **The Magi.** Depicted as Persian-attired figures (trousers and Phrygian caps marking their Eastern origin), labeled by Latin inscriptions: *SCS BALTHASSAR*, *SCS MELCHIOR*, *SCS GASPAR*. They carry their three gifts in offering posture. Their leading position in the procession reflects their canonical priority as the first Gentile worshippers of Christ (Matthew 2:1–12).
- **Mary's purple robes.** The Theotokos wears imperial purple robes — Byzantine imperial color, signifying her status as the throne-bearer of the King.
- **Date:** c. 561, under Bishop Agnellus of Ravenna (r. 556–569). The procession in the lower register was added/redesigned during Agnellus's reconsecration of the basilica from Arian (Theodoric's original 504 dedication) to Catholic following Justinian's reconquest of Italy. Some elements of the original Theodoric program may underlie the current procession; current consensus dates the procession in its current form to c. 556–569.
- **Medium:** Wall mosaic on gold ground.
- **Location:** Basilica of Sant'Apollinare Nuovo, Ravenna. UNESCO World Heritage Site (1996, "Early Christian Monuments of Ravenna"). Same site as #13 Christ Enthroned. Approx. 44.4172° N, 12.2034° E.
- **Era:** Early Byzantine.
- **Period:** Early Byzantine (Justinianic / early Exarchate of Ravenna). The mid-6th-century redecoration is contemporary with the Sinai Pantocrator (#2, c. 550) and slightly later than the apse of San Vitale (consecrated 547).

Key contextual facts that shaped the reflection:
- **Parallel composition.** Sant'Apollinare Nuovo's two facing wall programs (male martyrs to Christ on the south; female martyrs and Magi to the Theotokos on the north) form a coordinated theological argument: the entire church — both sexes, Jew and Gentile, martyr and patriarch — converges on the incarnate Lord. The Theotokos is the throne in the north program because Christ is depicted in his early infant body (the Magi visit a young child); the Christ Enthroned in the south program shows him in adult majesty (martyrs offering crowns to the cosmocrat).
- **Magi as first Gentile worshippers.** Matthew 2:1–12 records the Magi as the *first* Gentile worship of the incarnate Christ. Patristic tradition reads them as types of all Gentile believers — wise men of the nations turning to Israel's Messiah. The female-martyr procession behind them in the mosaic extends this typological reading: the Gentile church of all centuries follows the Magi to the throne.
- **Matthew 2:11 grammar.** The Greek (and KJV English) is unambiguous: the Magi worshipped *him* (the child), not Mary, and presented gifts to *him*. This is one of the cleanest scriptural pivots available against any reading of the Magi-and-Mary scene as Marian veneration. The reflection makes this grammatical point explicit.

## 4. Scripture verification (KJV)

The Greek text is ἔπεσαν προσεκύνησαν αὐτῷ (*epesan prosekynēsan autō*) — "fell down [and] worshipped him" — with the dative singular masculine pronoun *autō* clearly referring to the child (paidion, παιδίον, neuter — but the dative pronoun *autō* is determined by the antecedent grammatical hierarchy and the *kyrios* / *paidion* contextual focus). αὐτῷ here is dative singular masculine, referring to the παιδίον (child, neuter). ## 6. Open questions

- **Greek grammar correction.** I used "accusative" in the original draft but the Greek is dative. Correcting in the JSON to "masculine and singular — pointing to the child, not the mother." Substantively correct, technically more careful.
- **"Magi-content."** I used the phrase "preaching Magi-content." This is a Pastor Charlie idiom invention — meaning "what the Magi preached by their action." If you find it too casual or too clever, easy to swap for "Magi-style preaching" or "the Magi's message."
- **Star-charts and pagan training.** The phrase "foreigners with star-charts and pagan training" is direct about the Magi's actual social/religious identity (Persian Zoroastrian astrologers). Some readers may be surprised by the frankness ("pagan training"). My read: it's historically accurate and lands the missiological point without softening. The Magi were not Israelites; they came from outside the covenant and they came anyway.
