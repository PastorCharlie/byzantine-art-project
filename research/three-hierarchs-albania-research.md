## Quick reference

- **Object:** 14th-c. Late Byzantine / Palaeologan-era icon of the Three Hierarchs. National Historical Museum, Tirana, Albania. CC BY-SA 4.0 (Sailko 2015). 1720×2636 px.
- **Subjects:** Basil the Great (c. 330–379); John Chrysostom (c. 347–407); Gregory of Nazianzus the Theologian (c. 329–390).
- **Feast:** January 30, instituted in 1100 specifically to honor the three together.

## Calibration decisions

- **Three Hierarchs entry consolidates BRIEF Coll 7 #8, #9, #10** (Basil, Chrysostom, Gregory the Theologian) into a single entry. Iconographically and theologically these three are inseparable; treating them in one entry is more faithful to the tradition than three separate hagiographies.
- **Tier 2 Patristic — affirm warmly.** Per the locked-in three-tier Patristic list (batch 009 review), Basil, Chrysostom, and Gregory of Nazianzus are Tier 2: doctrinally aligned with GLM on Trinity, Christology, incarnation. The reflection treats their teaching as part of the patrimony GLM gladly receives.
- **Distinguishing angle:** **theologians, not warriors or martyrs.** Different case-type from George/Theodores. The reflection emphasizes their iconographic difference (books, not swords) and their canonical role (teaching, faith-handing-down).
- **Hebrews 13:7 anchor.** *"Remember them which have the rule over you, who have spoken unto you the word of God: whose faith follow."* — direct biblical instruction for handling teachers in the cloud of witnesses.
- **What their lives cost them:** Basil's famine-relief and hospital; Gregory's resignation of the Constantinople patriarchate over corruption; Chrysostom's exile under Eudoxia and death on the road. The reflection notes Chrysostom's last recorded words: *"Glory to God for all things."*
- **Named-decline applied (lighter):** *"We do not pray to Basil or Gregory or Chrysostom. We do not light candles to them as patrons of theological inquiry or of preaching."* But also *"we read what they wrote. We learn from how they taught. And we honor what their lives cost them."* The Tier 2 framing keeps the rejection from being dismissive.
- **Closing imperative:** *"When you preach Christian doctrine, do not preach it as if you invented it. The Three Hierarchs handed it to you. Hand it to the next generation."* — direct application of 2 Timothy 2:2.
